## Custom Project

### Dataset
The Dataset I used in my custom project was the second clinic_data provided by our professor. I named it `clinic_data_karlidean` for ease of understanding. This dataset took patients and called out their age and height. All patients are over 18 in this dataset.

### Signals
There were no new signals created, and I used the example ones. I made edits to the logic to call out how I used minimums instead of maximums for my boundaries.

### Experiments
When I was modifying this script, I kept receiving the entire dataset back when looking through my outputs. I learned about using the ampersand in my script so I can use multiple functions in tandem, as opposed to writing big nested functions that could be messy.\
In this experiment, I ended up showing a list of patients who were at least 35 years old and stood at least 5'6" tall.

### Results
I received 12 records in my output, all of patients who fit the bill of being at least 35 years old and 66 inches tall (5'6").

### Interpretation
In a business setting, this could help a company define insights and what kinds of markets they should be leaning into for sales growth. By changing the logic or applying different operators, we are able to see different kinds of demographics for this group. The way I had initially understood the data was similar to that of a doctor's office, but now I can see this as more of clothing sales or the like.
